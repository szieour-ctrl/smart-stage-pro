What AB 723 actually requires

Virtual staging isn't the problem. Silence about it is.

California's AB 723 doesn't say you can't digitally stage or alter a listing photo. It says that if you do, buyers get to know — and get to see the real, unaltered version too.

That means every digitally altered photo needs two things sitting next to it: a plain statement that it's been altered, and a working link or QR code to the original.

  • Applies to any digitally altered image — virtual staging, added or removed elements, edited finishes
  • The disclosure has to be reasonably conspicuous, on or adjacent to the photo
  • The link has to actually lead to the real, unaltered original
B&P Code §10140.8(a)(1)
"...shall include a statement disclosing that the image has been altered and a link to a publicly accessible internet website, URL, or QR code that includes, and clearly identifies, the original, unaltered image."
