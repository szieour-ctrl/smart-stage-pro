Why this matters for builders

Model home photos are real. That's exactly the problem.

Using real, unaltered photos of your model home to market an inventory unit is completely standard practice — and it's not what AB 723 is about. AB 723 covers digitally altered images: virtual staging, added furniture, edited finishes.

But MetroList has a separate, broader rule that catches model home photos specifically — and it's easy to miss because it isn't the same statute agents are used to hearing about.

MetroList Rule 12.10(f)
"Any image (including architectural renderings) that illustrates any condition other than the 'as-is' condition of the property shall be identified as such in the Public Remarks."
MetroList MLS Rules — True Picture Standard of Conduct
AB 723 · B&P Code §10140.8(a)(1)
Requires a disclosure statement and a link or QR code to the original, unaltered image — specifically for digitally altered photos.
